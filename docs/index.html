Overview

StudioLive MIDI Controller bridges your DAW and your PreSonus StudioLive III mixer. Move a fader in Logic Pro (or any DAW with MIDI output) and the physical mixer fader moves in real time â€” and vice versa. You can also trigger mute/solo from pads or buttons on any MIDI controller.

It is not a replacement for Universal Control â€” it works alongside it, adding DAW-driven automation and remote control that Universal Control doesn't provide.

Mute Groups

Mute groups are defined on the mixer itself (in Universal Control). The app reads them and shows a button for each active group in the toolbar.

  • Click a mute-group button to toggle muting of all assigned channels simultaneously.
  • Right-click a mute-group button to map it to a MIDI pad or note.
  • The button glows when the group is active (at least one channel muted by the group).

Logic Pro Setup

Logic Pro exposes a built-in virtual MIDI port called Logic Pro Virtual Out that is visible to all CoreMIDI apps on the same Mac â€” no extra setup required.

Step 1 â€” Configure Logic tracks

  1. For each mixer channel, create an External MIDI track in Logic.
  2. In the track Inspector, set MIDI Out to Logic Pro Virtual Out and pick a unique MIDI Channel (1â€“16) per mixer channel.
  3. Draw CC 7 (Volume) automation on the track, or record fader moves during playback.

Step 2 â€” Connect the app

  1. Click the MIDI status dot in the app header to open the MIDI panel.
  2. Select Logic Pro Virtual Out from the device list and click Connect.

Step 3 â€” Add mappings

  1. Right-click a fader â†’ Learn MIDI mapping, then press Play in Logic and move the corresponding fader to auto-detect CC and channel.
  2. Alternatively, right-click â†’ Edit Mapping and enter the CC number and MIDI channel manually.
  3. The MIDI channel in the mapping must match the channel set on the Logic track.

For the advanced MIDI Environment approach (a visual fader panel inside Logic) see the Logic Quick Reference.

ImportantMIDI channel numbers in Logic must match the channels in the app's mappings. Mismatched channels are the most common reason faders don't respond.

Troubleshooting

Mixer not found during discovery

  • Ensure the mixer and your Mac are on the same subnet.
  • Check firewall â€” allow port 53000 (UDP and TCP).
  • Try entering the IP manually in the Mixer connection panel.

MIDI not working

  • Check the MIDI status dot in the header â€” it must be green.
  • Open the MIDI Log and verify messages arrive when you move a knob.
  • Confirm MIDI channels match between the DAW track and the mapping.

Faders not responding (DAW â†’ Mixer)

  • Both mixer and MIDI dots must be green.
  • Verify the mapping uses the correct CC and MIDI channel.
  • In Logic, confirm the track's MIDI Out is set to Logic Pro Virtual Out (track Inspector).

Jerky fader movement

  • Increase Fader Smoothing in Preferences (try 400â€“500 ms).
  • Use a wired network connection rather than Wi-Fi.

Level dots always grey

  • The mixer meter stream requires an active connection â€” check the mixer status dot.
  • Play audio through the mixer and confirm signal is present on the physical meters.

Profile not loading

  • Check the preset directory: ~/Library/Application Support/StudioLive Midi Controller/
  • Verify the JSON is valid (open in a text editor).

Fader border pulsing red/white

  • A pulsing dashed red/white border means the MIDI device assigned to that fader is not currently connected.
  • Reconnect the MIDI device, or open Preferences â†’ MIDI Device Colors and remove the color assignment to clear the animation.

Level meter not working

  • Open Preferences and confirm Level Display is set to Indicator or Meter (not None).
  • Level data requires an active mixer connection â€” check the status bar dot is green.
  • Play audio through the mixer and verify signal on the physical meters; the app only shows levels when the mixer is streaming meter data.
